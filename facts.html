The Yuxi Circle: The World's Most Populated Area

The Yuxi Circle is the most densely populated area in the world, with 4.32 billion people living within a 4,000 km radius. This circle encompasses parts of China, India, Bangladesh, ASEAN countries, and some regions of Korea and Japan.

More people live inside this circle than outside, and it is home to over 4 million people. This region is significant for its fertile land, temperate climate, and its historical importance as the birthplace of human civilization.

Why is the population so high in this area?

  • Large rivers and plains for agricultural production.
  • A climate that is neither too hot nor too cold for human life.
  • Historically, this is where human civilization began.

Latest Facts and Data on Global Population

Global Population Growth

The global population is expected to reach 9.7 billion by 2050, representing a significant challenge for resources, food security, and environmental sustainability. This increase is largely driven by population growth in Asia and Africa.

Source: United Nations Population Division, 2023

Urbanization Trends

By 2030, over 60% of the global population is expected to live in urban areas, leading to challenges in urban planning, infrastructure, and environmental sustainability. Most urban growth will occur in cities in developing countries.

Source: UN-Habitat, 2023

Impact on Resources

As populations grow, global food production and water demand are predicted to increase by up to 50% by 2050. This will put further stress on ecosystems, biodiversity, and sustainable resource use.

Source: FAO (Food and Agriculture Organization), 2023
